Target patient profile:
The reader is a layperson without specialized medical knowledge. 
The main barriers are unfamiliar medical terminology, missing background knowledge, and unclear practical implications.
 They do not need overly-simplified information.

Checklist-based instructions:
- Identify medical terms, technical expressions, and abbreviations that may be difficult for a reader without medical background knowledge.
- Explain necessary medical terms in familiar everyday language.
- Clarify abbreviations and acronyms when they may be misunderstood by a non-expert reader.
- Replace unnecessary medical jargon with simpler expressions where possible.
- Identify concepts that assume medical background knowledge.
- Add short background explanations for important medical concepts.
- Make important medical relationships and mechanisms explicit, such as how symptoms, risks, diagnoses, or treatments are connected.
- Use examples or comparisons where they support understanding.
- Clarify what the information means for the patient’s own situation.
- Make possible options and consequences explicit when they are present in the source text.
- State warnings and next steps clearly.
- Indicate which points should be discussed with a healthcare professional.
